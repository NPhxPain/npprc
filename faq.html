  • 1. How do I prepare for a treatment?

    Our treatments have proven to be safe and easy. If possible and safe, we encourage you to stop pain medications, including over the counter pain medications, 8 hours in advance. Stop blood thinners 48 hours in advance. Please discuss this with your prescribing doctor prior to discontinuing blood thinners to be sure it is safe for you to do so. Having a small snack prior to your treatments may also help avoid decreases in blood sugar during, or after your treatment.

  • 2. How long will my appointments take?

    Depending on what specific treatments you are partaking in, most appointments last on average 30 minutes.

  • 3. What can I expect after the procedure?

    Everyone is different, but it is advisable to take it easy for the day of the procedure. It is helpful to apply ice to the affected area. After the first day you can perform activity as tolerated or as instructed by your physician. You should be able to return to work the next day unless otherwise instructed by your physician.

    Most patients report an immediate pain relief due to the local anesthetic. This will last a few hours. Your pain may return and then continue to lessen over the next few days as the anti-inflammatory has time to work. You may also feel some discomfort at the treatment site that will subside quickly.

  • 4. How long will the relief last?

    Every patient is unique. Some patients gain days of relief while other experience months of relief. It is all dependent upon your health history and severity of your condition.

  • 5. Is the treatment covered by insurance?

    We are in network with main insurance plans which include: Medicare, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna and Humana. Your specific insurance coverage will always be laid out to you prior to starting treatment in our facility.

  • 6. Are your procedures painful?

    The medications and the techniques that are used in our facility are designed to produce as little pain as possible during a procedure. Most patients tell us that the procedures are painless, but everyoneâ€™s pain tolerance is different. Some describe the procedure as simple pressure, some as being snapped by a rubber band or a slight sting and others claim no pain at all. It is our goal to make the treatment a positive, safe and effective experience.

  • 7. Do you use Steroids in your pain injection treatments?

    Many of our treatments do include a low dose steroid. If steroid is going to be used, the lowest and safest dose will be calculated to ensure maximum health benefits with minimal to no side effects. If there are concerns with the use of steroids, a patient can always discuss these with their physician or medical provider and decide what is best for them.
